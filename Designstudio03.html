Design Studio 03

Creating a collective story and narrative

Interesting design seminar focussed on storytelling and creating a narrative given by Laura. After completing the design intervention on Sunday it was cool to learn more about the philosophical side of design and biology. we first started with a lecture about different philosophers and how these philosophies play a role in how we see and understand the world. Then came the fun bit for me. We got into understanding narratives and storytelling. 

Personal Narrative

We had to each have an object/picture that represented our place in space and time but also how we got there and are part of a connected web. I chose my ring from my great aunt. It connects me to my family history and represents both loss and hope. It gives me a sense of belonging and support from my parents and siblings. It also highlights the differences between my two sides of the family, one side coming from the Netherlands and my maternal grandmother who moved from Spain to the Netherlands. 

Creating a Collective Story

We aWe each went around and talked how we saw a material/exercise we did in the past weeks and how it relates to us as a class. I started with a reference to making bio materials from coffee with a small group and how that related to everyone as we all get coffee collectively during breaks and it is a moment to come together. From here everyone chimed in and each time the red string was passed along. In the end we ended up with a large web of string connecting us all to each other. This was an interesting concept and something I immediately related to the red string in mythology, which binds people together. i found it interesting as we are a group of seemingly random people who all ended up choosing this one masters out of the hundred possibilities and in this one year. With that we have formed a community and it is interesting to see how we continue to grow closer.

Design Studio 03 Reflection

This Design studio class given by Laura was interesting to me. It allowed me to look at the world of design and storytelling through a philosophers perspective. It gave new insight into how philosophy can shape our perception of the world.

Next to this, the exercises we did in class helped me better understand how an individual story can be part of a collective. From first creating my personal narrative and then seeing how a personal object connects me to many others, and then working as a class to create a web of stories. How has something in the past weeks of MDEF effected me and how do others relate to this. It was a cool exercise and gave me a new view on how my classmates have felt during this course.

Finally during the writing of the collective story it was cool to see the story take shape through the joint effort of the class. It was fun as you did not really have control of the story you could only try and help steer when you had to write one or two sentences and the rest was out of your hands. Which is often the case when working in a group.
